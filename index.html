Why fish? What to look for.

Fish delivers high‑quality protein, omega‑3s (EPA/DHA), vitamin D, and B12. Choose smart to get benefits while managing risks like mercury, bones, and overfishing.

Look
Clear eyes, shiny scales, firm flesh that springs back.
Smell
Fresh sea breeze—never sour or ammonia‑like.
Gills
Bright cherry red; avoid brown or slimy.
Cold chain
Kept on ice; cook soon. Histamine risk for tuna/mackerel/sardines.

🐟 How to Choose Fresh Fish

👀 Eyes: Bright, bulging, not cloudy or sunken.
👃 Smell: Fresh sea breeze; avoid ammonia/acidic odors.
🎨 Gills: Cherry red; avoid brown or slimy.
🧪 Flesh: Springs back when pressed; fillet edges not browning.
🧊 Cold Chain: On ice, not submerged; ask when it arrived.
🏷️ Labels: Species, origin, farmed/wild, certifications (ASC/MSC) if available.

🧒 Kid‑Friendly Picks

For children, prioritize low‑mercury fish with few bones and gentle flavors. Serve in small, well‑cooked pieces and always check for pin bones.

Salmon ✅ Best

Low mercury · High omega‑3 · Fillet-friendly
  • Why good: Soft texture, easy to flake; omega‑3 for brain/eyes.
  • How to cook: Bake or steam; remove pin bones.

Seabass ✅ Best

Mild flavor · Few bones
  • Why good: Gentle taste, firm flakes, easy to de-bone.
  • How to cook: Steam or bake with minimal spice.

Tilapia ⚠️ Moderate

Low mercury · Budget‑friendly
  • Why consider: Mild taste; choose reputable farms.
  • Note: Lower omega‑3 than oily fish.
Be cautious with bones: Sardines and Rahu are nutritious but contain many small bones—serve only when meticulously prepared for younger kids.

🍽️ General Health Tips

  • Aim for 2 servings/week of fish; include at least one oily fish (sardine, mackerel, salmon).
  • Pregnant/breastfeeding: choose low‑mercury (sardine, salmon, tilapia, small mackerel); limit high‑mercury (large kingfish, tuna steaks).
  • Allergies: fish vs shellfish allergies differ; introduce cautiously.
  • Raw/undercooked: risk of parasites; use sushi‑grade or cook thoroughly.

🌍 Sustainability Note (UAE)

Some local species face heavy pressure. Hamour (grouper) is widely considered overfished; consider alternatives like seabass or sheri. Watch for seasonal bans (e.g., kingfish). Prefer certified farmed options (ASC/MSC) when possible.

🧭 Fish by Name

Kingfish (Kanad / Narrow‑barred Spanish mackerel) ⚠️ Moderate

Local: Kanad · English: Kingfish / Spanish mackerel
Details
✅ Positive:
  • Meaty steaks for grill/BBQ.
  • Good protein, B12, selenium, omega‑3.
⚠️ Concerns:
  • Mercury higher in large fish—limit for kids/pregnancy.
  • Seasonal catch rules; sustainability varies.
🍴 Best cooking: Grill, BBQ, curry, fry.
🦴 Bones: Few big bones (steak vertebra).
💪 Nutrients: Protein, omega‑3, B12, selenium.
⛔ Risks: Mercury; scombroid (histamine) if poorly chilled.

Sheri (Emperor Bream) ⚠️ Moderate

Local: Sheri · English: Emperor bream
Details
✅ Positive:
  • Mild, slightly sweet; family-friendly.
  • Versatile: whole or fillet.
⚠️ Concerns:
  • Moderate bones; check size/season rules.
  • Sustainability varies by stock.
🍴 Best cooking: Grill, bake, curry, fry.
🦴 Bones: Moderate; fillet-friendly.
💪 Nutrients: Protein, B12, selenium; omega‑3 moderate.
⛔ Risks: Small bones (choking hazard for kids).

Hamour (Grouper) ⛔ Environmental

Local: Hamour · English: Grouper
Details
✅ Positive:
  • Delicate, mild flavor; firm flakes.
  • Few bones; easy for kids.
⚠️ Concerns:
  • Overfishing locally—consider alternatives.
  • Occasional mislabeling; verify source.
🍴 Best cooking: Grill, pan-sear, curry, steam.
🦴 Bones: Few big bones; fillet/steak friendly.
💪 Nutrients: Protein, B12, potassium; omega‑3 moderate.
⛔ Risks: Environmental pressure; rare ciguatera risk in large reef fish.

Sardines ✅ Best

Local: Sardin · English: Sardines
Details
✅ Positive:
  • Very high omega‑3; budget-friendly.
  • Edible bones add calcium & vitamin D.
⚠️ Concerns:
  • Many small bones (often edible when cooked well).
  • Strong aroma; ultra-freshness matters.
🍴 Best cooking: Fry, grill, curry.
🦴 Bones: Many small bones.
💪 Nutrients: Protein, omega‑3, calcium, vitamin D, B12.
⛔ Risks: Scombroid (histamine) if mishandled.

Mackerel ⚠️ Moderate

Local: Bayah / Ayala (varies) · English: Mackerel
Details
✅ Positive:
  • High omega‑3; rich taste; affordable.
  • Great for quick fries and curries.
⚠️ Concerns:
  • Histamine risk if not kept cold.
  • Mercury depends on species (avoid large king mackerel).
🍴 Best cooking: Fry, grill, curry, smoke.
🦴 Bones: Many small bones; filleting helps.
💪 Nutrients: Protein, omega‑3, vitamin D, B12, selenium.
⛔ Risks: Histamine; mercury (species-dependent).

Seabass ✅ Best

Often barramundi or European seabass
Details
✅ Positive:
  • Mild, flaky; great for kids.
  • Farmed options with decent omega‑3 (choose ASC/MSC).
⚠️ Concerns:
  • Quality varies by farm; ask source/certification.
  • Price higher than tilapia.
🍴 Best cooking: Steam, bake, grill, pan-sear.
🦴 Bones: Few; fillet-friendly.
💪 Nutrients: Protein, omega‑3 (moderate), B12, vitamin D.
⛔ Risks: Farm chemical concerns if poorly regulated; microplastics.

Tilapia ⚠️ Moderate

Commonly farmed
Details
✅ Positive:
  • Very affordable; mild taste; widely available.
  • Low mercury.
⚠️ Concerns:
  • Lower omega‑3 vs oily fish.
  • Farm practices vary—prefer certified sources.
🍴 Best cooking: Fry, curry, bake, grill.
🦴 Bones: Fillet-friendly; few small bones.
💪 Nutrients: Protein, B12, selenium.
⛔ Risks: Farming chemicals/antibiotics (poorly regulated sources).

Rahu (Indian Carp) ⚠️ Moderate

Local: Rahu/Rohu · English: Indian carp
Details
✅ Positive:
  • Rich taste for curries/biryani; economical.
⚠️ Concerns:
  • Many fine bones; take care with children.
  • Often pond‑farmed; quality varies.
🍴 Best cooking: Curry, biryani, fry.
🦴 Bones: Many small bones.
💪 Nutrients: Protein, B12; omega‑3 low‑moderate.
⛔ Risks: Bones (choking), farm chemical concerns.

  • Delicate, buttery flesh; family‑friendly.
  • Few medium bones; easy to eat.
⚠️ Concerns:
  • Pricey at times; availability can vary.
  • Mercury generally moderate; source matters.
🍴 Best cooking: Shallow‑fry, grill, steam, curry.
🦴 Bones: Few medium bones; fillet‑friendly.
💪 Nutrients: Protein, omega‑3 (moderate), B12.
⛔ Risks: Mercury (moderate), sustainability varies.

Silver Pomfret ⚠️ Moderate

Local: Paplet (silver) · English: Silver pomfret
Details
✅ Positive:
  • Very mild, clean flavor; popular for steaming.
⚠️ Concerns:
  • Pricey; often imported; seasonal supply.
  • Sustainability depends on fishery.
🍴 Best cooking: Steam, shallow‑fry, curry.
🦴 Bones: Few medium bones; fillet‑friendly.
💪 Nutrients: Protein, omega‑3 (moderate), B12.
⛔ Risks: Mercury (moderate), supply variability.

Salmon ✅ Best

Commonly farmed Atlantic salmon
Details
✅ Positive:
  • Very high omega‑3; vitamin D powerhouse.
  • Versatile fillets; family‑friendly flavor.
⚠️ Concerns:
  • Farm practices vary—choose ASC/MSC when possible.
  • Allergies; raw requires sashimi‑grade handling.
🍴 Best cooking: Bake, grill, pan‑sear, steam; sushi/sashimi if safe source.
🦴 Bones: Fillet-friendly; remove pin bones.
💪 Nutrients: Protein, omega‑3 (high), vitamin D, B12, selenium.
⛔ Risks: Parasites in wild if eaten raw; farm contaminants if poorly regulated.

Tuna ⛔ Mercury

Species vary: Skipjack (lower mercury), Yellowfin (moderate), Albacore/Bigeye (higher)
Details
✅ Positive:
  • Meaty steaks; high protein; versatile.
⚠️ Concerns:
  • High mercury in larger species—limit for kids/pregnancy.
  • Histamine risk if warm‑handled.
  • Sustainability varies—prefer skipjack or certified sources.
🍴 Best cooking: Grill, sear, curry; sushi/sashimi only from trusted sources.
🦴 Bones: Fillet-friendly; few bones.
💪 Nutrients: Protein, omega‑3, B12, selenium.
⛔ Risks: Mercury (notably in big tuna); histamine.

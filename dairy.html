Dairy Buying Guide for UAE Retail

Grounded in Codex milk standards, GCC labelling rules, and UAE fortification policies. Covers cow, camel, goat dairy and plant-based alternatives across fat levels, probiotic counts, and handling requirements.

Label checkpoints

  • Milk fat %, non-fat solids %, and pasteurisation/UHT method (UAE.S GSO 04:2017).
  • Fortification levels for vitamin A & D (mandatory in UAE fluid milk).
  • For yoghurt/laban, declared live cultures and CFU count if marketing probiotics.
  • Cheese labels must show aging period, moisture %, and salt content.

Cheese styles & salt awareness

Fresh cheese (Feta, Nabulsi)

Nutrients & benefits

  • Provides protein, calcium, and tangy flavour for salads and mezze.
  • Brined varieties offer probiotic cultures when unpasteurised.

Best use

  • Rinse briefly to adjust salt; store submerged in fresh brine.
  • Consume within five days once opened for peak texture.

Risks & watch-outs

  • High sodium (1.6-2.5 g/100 g); portion carefully for low-salt diets.
  • Check PDO markings or declared milk type to confirm authenticity.

Hard cheese (Parmesan, Grana)

Nutrients & benefits

  • Aged 12+ months with concentrated calcium (~1150 mg/100 g) and 35 g protein.
  • Intense umami for grating over pasta, risotto, or salads.

Best use

  • Grate fresh to maintain aroma; store wedges wrapped in wax paper then foil.
  • Check rind stamping and cutting date on vacuum packs.

Risks & watch-outs

  • High sodium; balance portions within daily salt targets.
  • Trim surface mould that may form under plastic wrap.

Processed cheese slices

Advantages & nutrients

  • Convenient meltability for burgers and grilled sandwiches.

Best use

  • Store at or below 5 degC and finish within five days of opening.
  • Choose variants labelled "no added vegetable fat" if seeking pure dairy.

Risks & watch-outs

  • High sodium (1.2-1.4 g/100 g) and emulsifying salts; limit frequent use.
  • Check additive list complies with UAE.S 987:2015.

Plant-based alternatives

Nutrients & benefits

  • Fortified options can provide calcium and vitamin B12 similar to dairy.
  • Useful for lactose intolerance or vegan menus.

Best use

  • Select products fortified to at least 240 mg calcium per serving.
  • Use in cold applications where melting is less critical.

Risks & watch-outs

  • Coconut-based cheeses can carry high saturated fat; check labels.
  • Verify allergen declarations for soy, nuts, or gluten.

Cold-chain management

Retail expectations

Dubai Municipality requires dairy chillers at 0-5 degC with temperature log. Reject swollen UHT packs (possible spoilage) and verify tamper seals.

Home storage

Best practice

  • Keep milk on fridge shelves (not the door) to hold temp at or below 5 degC.
  • Wrap cheeses in wax paper then loose foil to allow airflow.

Risks & watch-outs

  • Opened UHT milk keeps 3-5 days refrigerated; discard when sour.
  • Avoid leaving dairy at room temperature for more than two hours.
Allergen labelling: Milk and lactose must be bolded in ingredient lists under UAE allergen rules. Plant alternatives must declare cross-contamination risks.

Health considerations

Lactose intolerance

Look for lactose-free milk (enzymatically treated) or try camel milk which naturally contains lower lactose.

Calcium intake

Adults require ~1000 mg/day (US National Academies 2023). Two cups of milk or laban plus cheese portion meets target.

Sodium moderation

Select low-salt labneh (<1.2%) and rinse brined cheeses to reduce sodium burden.

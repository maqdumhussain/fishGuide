Label checklist

  • Ensure bilingual labels and catch zone (FAO area) where required (UAE.S GSO 9:2015).
  • Look for handling instructions: keep at <=4 degC fresh, <=-18 degC frozen.
  • For prawns, confirm glazing % and sulphite declaration.
  • Ask for histamine testing certificates for scombroid fish (mackerel, tuna).

Sultan Ibrahim (Red Mullet)

Parupeneus heptacanthusLocal hook & line

Sweet, lean flesh with low mercury (<0.1 mg/kg). Small size limits bioaccumulation, making it ideal for weekly menus.

Nutrients & benefits

  • 19 g protein and about 250 mg EPA+DHA per 100 g (USDA FDC 2024).
  • Listed by EFSA 2014 as low contaminant thanks to short food chain.

Usage & prep

  • Best grilled whole, shallow fried, or oven roasted with light spices.
  • Score tail section and lift pin bones before serving to children.

Risks & watch-outs

  • Monitor freshness: clear eyes and firm scales signal proper icing.
  • Keep below 4 degC to avoid histamine formation.

Sheri (Spangled Emperor)

Lethrinus nebulosusLocal seasonal

Recommended by UAE sustainability programme when harvested under 40 cm. Medium flakes and versatile flavour.

Nutrients & benefits

  • Approx. 20 g protein and 190 mg EPA+DHA per 100 g serving.
  • Mercury averages 0.11 mg/kg, supporting two servings weekly for pregnancy.

Usage & prep

  • Ideal for machboos, stuffed roasts, or lemongrass steaming.
  • Request centre-cut fillets to simplify Y-bone removal.

Risks & watch-outs

  • Insist on icing within 2 hours to keep histamine low (Codex CAC/RCP 52).
  • Verify size compliance to protect spawning stock.

Farmed European Seabass

Dicentrarchus labraxASC-certified

Controlled aquaculture systems deliver dependable omega-3 content with tight antibiotic oversight.

Nutrients & benefits

  • About 1.2 g omega-3 per 150 g plus notable vitamin D and selenium.
  • ASC or GlobalG.A.P. certification signals low contaminant residue.

Usage & prep

  • Steam with citrus, pan sear, or salt-bake to highlight mild flavour.
  • Fillets contain few pin bones, simplifying kid-friendly servings.

Risks & watch-outs

  • Check harvest date and keep on perforated trays to prevent gaping.
  • Maintain 0-2 degC storage to avoid texture breakdown.

Local Prawns (Roobian)

Penaeus semisulcatusFlash-frozen

Flash-freezing within hours of harvest locks in muscle quality and omega-3s while keeping mercury minimal.

Nutrients & benefits

  • Delivers about 24 g protein and 190 mg EPA+DHA per 100 g.
  • Provides iodine and B12 for thyroid and energy metabolism.

Usage & prep

  • Suited to quick grills, curries, and stir-fries; avoid overcooking.
  • Prefer IQF packs with transparent glazing percentage.

Risks & watch-outs

  • Shellfish allergen; check for sulphite declarations on labels.
  • Reject frost build-up or thawed appearance signalling temperature abuse.

Silver Grunt (Shaari)

Pomadasys argenteusLow mercury

Mild, kid-friendly fillets with reliable low-contaminant data from GCC monitoring.

Nutrients & benefits

  • Low mercury (under 0.09 mg/kg) and good protein density.
  • Suitable for school menus and pregnancy due to gentle flavour.

Usage & prep

  • Pan fry, shape into kofta, or simmer in stews.
  • Few bones; simple to portion for children.

Risks & watch-outs

  • Cook within 24 hours of purchase; flesh softens if held longer.
  • Keep chilled at 0-2 degC to maintain texture.

Small Sardines

Sardinella longicepsIndian Ocean purse seine

Small pelagic fish delivering significant omega-3 and calcium from edible bones.

Nutrients & benefits

  • About 2.2 g omega-3 and high calcium per 100 g serving.
  • EFSA recognises sardines as low-risk for prenatal nutrition.

Usage & prep

  • Pressure cook or butterfly to soften bones for children.
  • Available fresh or canned; choose low-sodium tins under 350 mg/100 g.

Risks & watch-outs

  • Maintain strict cold chain to avoid histamine spikes.
  • Rinse canned sardines if salt-sensitive.

Kanad (Narrow-barred Spanish Mackerel)

Scomberomorus commersonPopular grilled

Iconic UAE grilling fish with dense flesh and strong omega-3 profile, though mercury accumulates in large fish.

Nutrients & benefits

  • Roughly 1.8 g omega-3 per 100 g and rich vitamin B12 content.
  • Firm texture holds up on grill and in curries.

Usage & prep

  • Cut into steaks for grill or smoke; marinate to curb surface bacteria.
  • Select fish under 8 kg for sweeter flesh and lower mercury.

Risks & limits

  • Mercury ranges 0.3-0.7 mg/kg; limit to one adult portion weekly and twice monthly for pregnancy.
  • Demand catch date and proper icing to prevent histamine spikes.

Hamour (Orange-spotted Grouper)

Epinephelus coioidesReef species

Traditional Emirati favourite with soft flakes but significant sustainability pressure in the Gulf.

Nutrients & benefits

  • Supplying lean protein with moderate omega-3 (about 0.6 g/100 g).
  • Mild taste suits family meals and picky eaters.

Usage & prep

  • Steam or bake to retain moisture; avoid over-frying.
  • Consider farmed barramundi as a texture match when possible.

Risks & limits

  • IUCN lists species as Vulnerable; limit purchase of wild-caught stocks.
  • Mercury around 0.2-0.35 mg/kg; choose smaller fish and verify origin certificates.

Tilapia (Farmed)

Oreochromis niloticusRAS farms

Economical choice grown in recirculating systems; dependable protein with modest omega-3.

Nutrients & benefits

  • About 20 g protein per 100 g with low saturated fat.
  • RAS production limits off-flavours when farms meet GlobalG.A.P. standards.

Usage & prep

  • Bake, pan fry, or steam with spices; absorbs marinades well.
  • Combine with omega-3 rich sides such as flax, chia, or nuts.

Risks & watch-outs

  • Low omega-3 (approx. 0.2 g/100 g); diversify seafood intake.
  • Verify GlobalG.A.P. or ASC certification to avoid muddy flavours.

Yellowfin Bream

Acanthopagrus latusRegional catchFirm flesh

Sturdy fillets suited to grilling with relatively low mercury when caught under size limits.

Nutrients & benefits

  • Provides lean protein with modest omega-3 and selenium.
  • Bone structure manageable for family servings when filleted.

Usage & prep

  • Grill whole with lemon butter or bake; score skin to prevent curling.
  • Select fish under 1 kg for sweeter flesh.

Risks & watch-outs

  • Mercury stays below 0.2 mg/kg when smaller fish are chosen; limit large specimens.
  • Follow MOCCAE seasonal size advisories to support stocks.

Large Tuna (Yellowfin/Bigeye)

Thunnus spp.Imported

High omega-3 predator fish but significant methylmercury burdens require strict portion control.

Nutrients & benefits

  • Excellent protein and omega-3 levels for occasional meals.
  • Rich in selenium and vitamin B12.

Usage & prep

  • Sear quickly or enjoy as tartare only from certified sashimi-grade suppliers.
  • Insist on -18 degC storage records for frozen blocks.

Risks & limits

  • Mercury reaches 0.4-1.0 mg/kg; avoid for pregnancy and children, adults keep to rare occasions.
  • Histamine hazards increase if the cold chain is broken.

Shark and Ray Cuts (Safaidi)

Various speciesBycatch

Often sold as generic steaks but typically carry high mercury, ammonia odour, and legal restrictions.

Nutrients & benefits

  • No substantial benefit over safer white fish options.

Usage & prep

  • Not recommended; choose cobia or barramundi for similar firmness.

Risks & limits

  • Mercury may exceed 1.5 mg/kg and urea causes strong ammonia odour.
  • Cabinet Decision 500/2017 protects many shark species; trade is heavily restricted.

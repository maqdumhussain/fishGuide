Egg Buying Guide for UAE Retail

Combines UAE Food Code, ESMA egg grading scheme, and FAO nutrient data to explain shell stamps, storage, and health considerations for regular, DHA-enriched, organic, and pasteurised eggs.

Carton checklist

  • Grade (A, B), size class (S, M, L, XL) per UAE.S 997:2018.
  • Farm registration number and country of origin.
  • Production date + best-before (typically 21 days chilled).
  • Storage instructions: keep at <=5 degC; display case must be refrigerated.

Understanding shell stamps

Format example: AE-02-1234

  • AE - Country code (United Arab Emirates).
  • 02 - Production system (02 = barn, 03 = free-range, 04 = organic).
  • 1234 - Farm or packing station ID.
  • Stamped on each egg plus outer carton.

Colour-coded trays (large markets)

  • White tray - conventional caged eggs.
  • Green tray - free-range/organic (requires certification).
  • Yellow tray - DHA-enriched or omega-3 eggs (with label claim).
  • Blue tray - pasteurised shell eggs (food service).

Imported eggs

  • Must display importing company, veterinary certificate number, and halal statement.
  • MOCCAE inspects for Salmonella Enteritidis vaccination records.
  • Check that packing date + shipping time fits 21-day best-before.

Nutrition & health

Standard UAE eggs

Nutrients & benefits

  • Provide about 6.3 g complete protein per large egg.
  • Supply vitamin D (1.1 mcg) and choline for brain health.

Best use

  • Suitable for daily breakfasts or baking; cook until yolk thickens.
  • Select Grade A, size class as needed for recipe yields.

Risks & watch-outs

  • Contains about 186 mg cholesterol; align with AHA guidance of one egg/day for healthy adults.
  • Store at or below 5 degC to prevent Salmonella growth.

DHA-enriched eggs

Nutrients & benefits

  • Deliver roughly 120-150 mg DHA per egg when hens are flax/algae fed.
  • Useful for pregnancy, toddlers, and brain-support diets.

Best use

  • Use in daily meals to top up omega-3 without seafood.
  • Keep refrigeration consistent to protect delicate fatty acids.

Risks & watch-outs

  • Verify labelled DHA content per UAE.S 192:2014; not all enriched claims match.
  • Price premium; rotate with standard eggs to manage cost.

Organic / free-range

Nutrients & benefits

  • Comparable macronutrients to standard eggs with potential for higher omega-3.
  • Improved welfare standards and perceived flavour.

Best use

  • Great for poached or soft preparations where flavour matters.
  • Look for ESMA or recognised EU/US organic certification.

Risks & watch-outs

  • Higher price; plan menu costing accordingly.
  • Still require refrigeration and same food safety controls.

Pasteurised liquid eggs

Advantages & nutrients

  • Safe for no-cook dishes like mousse or mayonnaise.
  • Deliver the same protein as shell eggs with reduced pathogen risk.

Best use

  • Use in catering for desserts, sauces, and batching scrambled eggs.
  • Follow manufacturer guidance on shaking before use.

Risks & watch-outs

  • Refrigerate at or below 4 degC once opened and finish within three days.
  • Distinguish between UHT shelf-stable and chilled variants to avoid spoilage.

Safety & storage

Home storage

Best practice

  • Keep eggs in the original carton at or below 5 degC and store on fridge shelves.
  • Place pointed end down to centre the yolk.

Risks & watch-outs

  • Use within 21 days of pack date; hard-boiled eggs last one week refrigerated.
  • Discard cracked eggs to avoid bacterial entry.

Kitchen hygiene

Best practice

  • Wash hands and utensils after handling raw eggs.
  • Cook until yolks thicken unless using pasteurised product for soft dishes.

Risks & watch-outs

  • Discard cracked or dirty shells to block bacterial entry.
  • Separate raw egg prep from ready-to-eat foods to prevent cross contamination.
Temperature control: Dubai Municipality mandates egg displays between 2-5 degC. Reject cartons left unrefrigerated for more than 2 hours.
